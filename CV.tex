\section*{Research overview}

My research is mainly focused on the development of code and algorithms to efficiently sample properties of molecules and nanoclusters with intricate potential energy surface and/or embedded in solvent.
Specifically, I have experience with Monte Carlo methods and extended ensemble methods, such as replica exchange.
Recently, my work has been focused on the development of a Bias Monte Carlo method to sample internal degrees of freedom of molecules in solution, which is the main theme of my Ph.D. thesis.
To achieve the objectives of my research, my work rely on high performance computing, and for this reason, I also have interest in data structures and parallelism.
